Biography
Left Hand entered Season 1 as the presumed favorite — the dominant, coordinated limb with superior grip control and strategic patience. Early results confirmed the pre-season narrative. Left Hand opened with a strong win streak, establishing psychological dominance and a corner-anchoring playstyle that became its trademark. The mid-season challenges tested Left Hand's resolve. The comeback has been nothing short of legendary. Can it close out the championship?
Signature Moves
The Double Diagonal
Forces two simultaneous diagonal threats that cannot both be blocked. Left Hand's most reliable closer.
The Corner Anchor
Controls the bottom-left corner from move 1, building a fortress that Right Hand has failed to penetrate consistently.
The Patience Play
Deliberately slow openings that lure Right Hand into aggressive overextension, then punishes with surgical counter-strikes.

Biography
Right Hand is chaos incarnate. Written off early, it has stunned the league with dominant winning streaks — the longest run by either competitor. Right Hand's style is aggressive, center-column dominant, and wildly inconsistent. Brilliant one match, baffling the next. Currently fighting back in the standings, a comeback is never out of the question. The dramatic moments it has produced are the most talked about of Season 1.
Signature Moves
The Center Storm
Floods columns 3, 4, and 5 in the opening, creating a suffocating center presence that Left Hand consistently underestimates.
The Speed Run
Rapid-fire wins that don't allow Left Hand to establish its methodical setup. Pure attack with no defense.
The Trap Drop
Baits Left Hand into blocking a fake threat, then completes a second threat three columns away. Most spectacular move in the arsenal.
